HATHA YOGA

Hatha Yoga is a traditional form of physical yoga that emphasizes the practice of physical postures (asanas) along with breath control (pranayama) and meditation. The word "Hatha" is derived from two Sanskrit terms: "Ha," which means sun, and "Tha," which means moon. Together, they represent the balance of opposing forces, symbolizing the balance that practitioners seek to achieve in their bodies and minds.

Padmasana

Padmasana, commonly known as Lotus Pose, is a classic seated yoga posture that is widely used for meditation and pranayama (breath control exercises). It is a cross-legged position that resembles the petals of a lotus flower, symbolizing purity and enlightenment in various Eastern spiritual traditions.

Here are the steps to practice Padmasana:
1. Sit on the floor: Begin in a seated position on the floor with your legs extended straight in front of you.
2. Bend one knee: Bring one foot toward your pelvis, bending the knee. Place the sole of that foot on the inner thigh of the opposite leg.
3. Bend the other knee: Similarly, bring the other foot toward your pelvis and place the sole on the inner thigh of the first leg. Both knees should ideally rest on the floor.
4. Alignment: The heels should be close to your abdomen, and the soles of your feet should be facing upward. The spine should be straight, and your shoulders should be relaxed.
5. Hand placement: Place your hands on your knees, with palms facing upward. You can use the traditional mudra by touching the index finger to the thumb and keeping the other three fingers extended.
6. Gaze: Keep your gaze forward with your eyes closed, focusing on your breath or a point in front of you.

Padmasana is known for its ability to promote physical and mental stability, making it an ideal posture for meditation. It helps open up the hips and encourages proper spinal alignment. It's important to note that Padmasana requires flexibility in the hips and knees, so it may take time to achieve the full expression of the pose. If you have any knee or hip issues, it's advisable to approach this pose with caution or seek guidance from a yoga instructor.
